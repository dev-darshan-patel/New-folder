Bookify

A multi-tenant, Calendly-style appointment booking SaaS. A business owner signs up, sets weekly availability and "event types," and shares a public link where customers self-book a time slot — no back-and-forth emails.

What is Bookify

Bookify lets a small business (a consultant, salon, coach, agency — anyone who takes appointments) run their own scheduling page without touching code. Each signup becomes an independent tenant: their own availability, their own event types, their own booking page at bookify.app/{business-slug}, and their own customers. Tenants pick a plan (Free / Pro / Business) that unlocks more capability as they grow — everything from custom branding to team round-robin scheduling to accepting payments.

Above all tenants sits a platform super-admin console for the people running Bookify itself: user management, billing oversight, plan configuration, feature flags, and a full audit trail.

For business owners

Set your hours, create a bookable event, share one link. Customers pick a slot, get a confirmation email with a calendar invite, and can reschedule or cancel themselves — you never touch it.

For developers

Next.js 16 App Router, server-first, no REST/tRPC layer — data reads happen directly in server components, mutations go through server actions. Prisma + PostgreSQL. See Architecture.

Quick tour

  1. Sign up at /signup (email/password, or "Sign in with Google/Microsoft"). A starter event type and default Mon–Fri 9–5 availability are created automatically.
  2. Set availability at /dashboard/availability — a weekly grid, plus optional one-off date overrides ("closed July 20th", "extra hours this Saturday").
  3. Create or edit an event type at /dashboard/event-types — duration, location (in-person/phone/video), and (on paid plans) intake questions, scheduling limits, approval flow, and more.
  4. Share the link — /{your-slug}/{event-slug} — on your website, in your email signature, or embedded directly via the embed widget.
  5. A customer books — picks a time slot (shown in their own timezone), fills in name/email (+intake questions if configured), and confirms. Both parties get an email with an .ics calendar invite.
  6. Manage the booking — the customer's confirmation email includes a private link (/booking/{token}) to reschedule or cancel with no login required. You manage all bookings from /dashboard/bookings.

Bookify ships three default plans — Free, Pro, Business — but a platform admin can edit exactly which capabilities each plan grants (or create entirely new tiers) at any time from /admin/settings/plans, with changes taking effect immediately, no redeploy. Availability & event types

Availability

Set a recurring weekly schedule (e.g. Mon–Fri, 9:00–17:00) at /dashboard/availability. Add one-off date overrides for exceptions — mark a specific date fully closed, or replace its hours just for that day — without touching your regular weekly grid.

Event types

An event type is a bookable meeting (e.g. "30 Minute Intro Call"). Configure per event type:

  • Duration and location (in-person, phone, or auto-generated Google Meet / Zoom link)
  • Minimum notice and daily/weekly/monthly booking caps PRO
  • Intake questions shown on the booking form PRO
  • Manual approval — bookings wait for your OK before they're confirmed PRO
  • Confirmation redirect URL and a custom reply-to email PRO
  • Group sessions — a fixed-capacity class/webinar many people can book into BUSINESS
  • Recurring — invitees book a weekly series up to 8 occurrences at once BUSINESS
  • Team assignment — solo, round-robin, or collective across your team BUSINESS
  • Price — charge for the booking, once payments are approved and connected BUSINESS

Slots are always shown to the customer in their own detected timezone, converted from your business timezone automatically.

Bookings

See every upcoming and past booking at /dashboard/bookings. From here you can:

  • Approve or decline pending bookings (if the event type requires approval)
  • Create a manual booking for a phone/walk-in customer PRO
  • Export to CSV PRO

Customers manage their own bookings via a private link in their confirmation email (/booking/{token}) — no login needed. They can reschedule (re-checked against your live availability) or cancel, which instantly frees the slot for someone else. Every booking email includes a standard .ics calendar attachment that updates or removes itself in the customer's calendar app when you reschedule or cancel it.

Branding & embed

Customize your public booking page at /dashboard/branding: accent color, font, logo, and a welcome message. FREE

Embed your booking page directly into your own website at /dashboard/embed — a small copy-paste script tag (/public/embed.js) that injects an iframe or popup and auto-resizes to fit its content. Accent color and font overrides can be passed as script-tag attributes. FREE

Team scheduling

BUSINESS Add team members at /dashboard/team, each with their own availability. Assign an event type to:

  • Solo — just you (default)
  • Round-robin — bookings rotate evenly across the assigned team members
  • Collective — a slot only shows as available when every assigned member is free

Billing & payments

Your subscription

Manage your Bookify plan at /dashboard/billing — upgrade, downgrade, or open the Stripe customer portal to update your card or cancel. Your plan can be set to cancel at the end of the current billing period instead of immediately.

Accepting payments from your customers

BUSINESS To charge customers for a booking: apply for payments approval in /dashboard/settings, wait for admin review, then connect a provider (Stripe Connect or Razorpay Route, depending on your country) through a short hosted onboarding flow. Once connected, you can set a price on any solo (non-group, non-recurring) event type. Payment holds for 24 hours after the appointment before releasing to you, so a same-day refund is always possible.

Calendar sync

Connect your Google Calendar or Zoom account at /dashboard/settings:

  • Google Calendar — auto-generates a Google Meet link per booking, and (optionally) blocks your Bookify availability whenever you're busy on that calendar BUSINESS for busy-sync
  • Zoom — auto-generates a unique Zoom meeting link per booking PRO
Not built yet Outlook/Microsoft 365 calendar sync (busy-time blocking or event creation) isn't implemented — Microsoft OAuth today is only used for account sign-in.

Account & security

  • Two-factor authentication — enable TOTP (any authenticator app) plus one-time backup codes at /dashboard/settings/security.
  • Sign in with Google/Microsoft — link a social login to your existing account, or sign up fresh with one.
  • Account deletion — request deletion any time; you get a grace window with a one-click "keep my account" undo before anything is actually removed. Once the grace period lapses, there's still a 30-day recovery window via an emailed link before permanent purge.

Admin Guide

For platform operators — the people running Bookify itself, at /admin.

Admin roles

Access to /admin is granted per-account via adminRole (never self-serve — set directly in the database). Non-admins get a plain 404 on any /admin route, so the console's existence isn't revealed.

RoleCan do
SUPER_ADMINEverything below, including impersonation and permanent account deletion
SUPPORTDay-to-day operations: view/search users & bookings, notes, most mutating actions except the most destructive ones
READ_ONLYView everything, change nothing

Every mutating admin action is checked server-side (never just hidden in the UI) and writes an entry to the append-only audit log at /admin/audit.

Managing tenants

  • /admin/users — search/filter/sort/paginate every tenant; CSV export. Click into a user for their branding preview, weekly availability, booking history, Stripe customer link, and internal admin notes (never shown to the tenant).
  • /admin/bookings — cross-tenant bookings explorer with search/status/business/date filters, a stats strip (cancellation rate, reschedule rate), CSV export, and inline moderation (suspend a business directly from a row).
  • /admin overview — a full analytics dashboard (signups, bookings, approximate MRR-over-time) over a 7/30/90-day range.
  • Coupons, announcements, broadcast — promo codes for billing, a dashboard banner tenants see, and one-off broadcast messaging.

Platform settings

PageConfigures

/admin/settings/authGoogle / Microsoft "Sign in with…" OAuth app credentials
/admin/settings/platformMaintenance mode, signups on/off, support email

Danger zone

  • Impersonation SUPER_ADMIN only — log in as a tenant to see exactly what they see, with a visible banner and one-click "stop impersonating."
  • Suspend / unsuspend — blocks login and hides the tenant's public booking pages.
  • Soft delete — hides the account (restorable indefinitely).
  • Hard delete SUPER_ADMIN only — permanent, requires typing the account's slug to confirm.
  • Force password reset — emails the tenant a one-time reset link.

Architecture

Server-first, no API layer

Data is read directly in async server components via Prisma. Mutations go through server actions — there is intentionally no REST or tRPC layer. The only client components are the ones that genuinely need interactivity (auth forms, the copy-link button, the booking widget).

Multi-tenancy

Multi-tenancy is row-level by userId. Every EventType, Availability, and Booking belongs to a tenant. The public site keys off User.slug; every dashboard action re-checks ownership (where: { id, userId: user.id }) so one tenant can never touch another's rows. /admin is the one deliberate exception, reading across every tenant.

Auth

Sessions are never delegated to a third party, even when a user authenticates via Google or Microsoft. A signed JWT (src/lib/auth.ts) is issued and stored in an httpOnly session cookie regardless of login method. Passwords are bcrypt-hashed; OAuth-only accounts simply have no password hash and password login is rejected for them. "Sign in with Google/Microsoft" is a hand-rolled Authorization Code flow — not a library — that verifies the returned id_token's signature against the provider's real JWKS, and links to an existing account by verified email (never auto-links an unverified one).

Scheduling engine

The most delicate code in the app: availability is stored timezone-naively (a weekday + minutes-from-midnight), while bookings are stored in UTC. Slot generation converts the business's wall-clock availability into a UTC instant using the owner's IANA timezone, then filters out past slots and anything overlapping an existing booking. Double-booking is prevented twice: once when generating slots (booked times are hidden) and again at write time with an atomic overlap check immediately before insert.

Feature entitlements

A code registry (src/lib/features.ts) defines every gateable FeatureKey; the admin-editable Plan.featureKeys array is the actual source of truth for what each plan grants. planHasFeature(planId, key) in src/lib/plans.ts is the single read path every gate in the app calls — never a raw plan-name comparison. Downgrades never destroy existing data; gates only block creating new gated things (see the callout in Plans & features).

Email

Sends via SMTP if configured, otherwise logs the message to the server console — the app runs with zero email config out of the box. A booking always succeeds even if the confirmation email fails to send.

Conventions & gotchas

This is not the Next.js you know The project's own AGENTS.md flags that this Next.js version has breaking changes from what most training data assumes. Check node_modules/next/dist/docs/ before using an unfamiliar API.
  • Server actions only, never a REST layer — new mutations belong in an actions.ts file next to the page that uses them, marked "use server".
  • Always re-check ownership server-side — every dashboard mutation filters by userId: user.id, never trusting an ID from the client alone.
  • Store UTC, interpret in the owner's timezone — never change this invariant in src/lib/availability.ts without preserving both directions.
  • Feature gates: downgrade never destroys data — when adding a new gated field, make the "not entitled" branch fall back to the existing DB value, not a hardcoded default (see updateEventTypeAction in src/app/dashboard/actions.ts for the pattern).
  • Stripe/Razorpay test vs. live never mix — getActiveStripeConfig() reads only the active mode's fields, with no fallback to the other mode.
  • The manageToken pattern — any "do this without logging in" flow (booking manage, password reset, account recovery) uses a random, single-purpose, unguessable token embedded in an email link, not a login session.
  • UI hiding is a courtesy layer only — every gate hidden in a form must also be enforced in the server action that receives the submission.

Deployment

Vercel + Neon/Vercel Postgres

  1. Provision a Postgres database (Vercel Postgres via the dashboard, or Neon directly) — you'll get a pooled and an unpooled connection string.
  2. Set project env vars on Vercel: DATABASE_URL, DIRECT_URL, a fresh production AUTH_SECRET, NEXT_PUBLIC_APP_URL, a fresh CRON_SECRET. SMTP_*/STRIPE_* are optional fallbacks — real config lives in /admin/settings once deployed.
  3. Push to your Git remote and import the repo in Vercel.
  4. After the first deploy, run npx prisma migrate deploy once against the production database to create the schema.
  5. Visit /admin/settings and /admin/settings/email to configure Stripe and email credentials live — no redeploy needed.
  6. Manually promote your own account: connect to the production DB (e.g. npx prisma studio with production env vars) and set adminRole = SUPER_ADMIN on your row. There's no self-serve path for this by design.

Reminder cron

Booking reminders (24h + 1h before) are pull-based — nothing fires on its own. Something must hit /api/cron/reminders every few minutes, authenticated with CRON_SECRET as a bearer token or ?secret= query param. The endpoint is idempotent, so overlapping calls are safe.

Vercel Hobby plan limitation Hobby only allows once-daily cron schedules. The 10-minute schedule in vercel.json requires Vercel Pro. On Hobby, use a free external pinger (cron-job.org, UptimeRobot, a GitHub Actions schedule) hitting the same endpoint every few minutes instead.

FAQ & Troubleshooting

Common issues

Booking confirmation emails aren't arriving

Check whether SMTP_HOST (or the DB-backed provider at /admin/settings/email) is actually configured. With no email config at all, Bookify logs the email content to the server console instead of sending it — this is by design for zero-config local dev, but means "silently not sending" in production if nobody set it up.

"Sign in with Google/Microsoft" fails after changing the deployment URL

The OAuth redirect URI is registered separately on each provider's own developer console and does not update automatically when NEXT_PUBLIC_APP_URL changes. Update it on Google Cloud Console / Azure AD to match your current domain.

Embed widget or booking links show an old domain

Links and embed snippets are built from NEXT_PUBLIC_APP_URL at request time — update the env var on your hosting platform and redeploy.

Stripe payments behave differently than expected

Check stripeMode at /admin/settings — TEST and LIVE credentials are stored separately and never mixed. Confirm you're looking at the Stripe dashboard in the matching mode.

Reminder emails never send

Nothing triggers them on its own — confirm a scheduler (Vercel Cron or an external pinger) is actually hitting /api/cron/reminders and that CRON_SECRET matches. See Deployment.

A tenant lost access to a feature after I changed their plan

Expected if the new plan doesn't grant that FeatureKey — check /admin/settings/plans. Any data they already created should keep working; only creating new instances of the gated thing is blocked.
